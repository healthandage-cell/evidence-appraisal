**Appraised document:** Di Molfetta S, Di Gioia L, Caruso I, et al. *Efficacy and Safety of Different Hybrid Closed Loop Systems for Automated Insulin Delivery in People With Type 1 Diabetes: A Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis.* Diabetes/Metabolism Research and Reviews. 2024;40(6):e3842. DOI: 10.1002/dmrr.3842.
**Registration:** PROSPERO CRD42023453717
**Appraisal date:** 2026-07-10
**Tools applied:** PRISMA 2020 (+ PRISMA-NMA items) · ROBIS · AMSTAR-2

> **Routing note.** Document type detected = **systematic review with network meta-analysis (NMA)**. The correct reporting standard is **PRISMA-NMA (Hutton 2015)**, which extends PRISMA 2020 with items on network geometry, ranking, transitivity, and consistency. This report scores PRISMA 2020 and notes the NMA-specific items separately.
>
> **Scope note (updated 2026-07-10 — supplement reviewed).** The main article **and** Supporting Information S1 (full search strategy [S2], study/participant characteristics [S2.1–S2.2], per-study risk of bias [S3], comparison-adjusted funnel plots [S4], pairwise results and forest plots [S6], and GRADE/CINeMA certainty tables) have both been reviewed. All previously supplement-dependent items are resolved. **AMSTAR-2 items 4 and 7 are finalised below.**

## 1. Overall verdict at a glance

| Tool | Result | One-line basis |
|---|---|---|
| **PRISMA 2020** | **High reporting completeness** — ~24/27 items met, 3–4 partial, ~1 not reported | Structured, pre-registered, transparent methods; minor gaps in protocol-amendment reporting and itemised exclusions |
| **ROBIS** | **Low risk of bias** | Duplicate selection/extraction, RoB 2, rigorous NMA synthesis, cautious interpretation |
| **AMSTAR-2** | **Low confidence (confirmed)** — one critical item unmet: no list of excluded studies with reasons (item 7) exists in the article or its supplement | Methodologically strong review; the single unmet AMSTAR-2 critical domain caps the rating at Low |

**Key headline:** This is a **methodologically strong, well-reported review**. The ROBIS "Low risk of bias" reflects that. The AMSTAR-2 "Low confidence" is *not* a contradiction — it is the well-known behaviour of AMSTAR-2, where a single unmet **critical** domain (here, item 7: list of excluded studies with justification) caps the rating regardless of overall quality. Having now reviewed the supplement, the excluded-studies list is **genuinely absent**, so AMSTAR-2 is **confirmed Low** — a real (if minor) reporting gap, not an artefact of missing files. Had the authors included such a list (routine practice), the review would rate **Moderate–High**.

### AMSTAR-2 overall confidence: **LOW (confirmed)**

**Rating logic:** AMSTAR-2 = *Critically Low* if >1 critical flaw; *Low* if one critical flaw; *Moderate* if >1 non-critical weakness only; *High* if ≤1 non-critical weakness. Here, **item 7 (critical) is unmet**, which alone caps the rating at **Low** — even though every other critical domain (2 partial, 4 partial, 9, 11, 13, 15) is met or nearly met.

**Now confirmed (SI reviewed):** the excluded-studies list is genuinely absent from both the article and Supporting Information S1, so item 7 stays **No** and the rating is a definitive **Low**. Had the authors provided an excluded-studies list — routine practice — the review would rate **Moderate to High**. This single, minor omission is the only thing standing between a strong review and a strong AMSTAR-2 score.

## 5. Prioritised gaps (what to fix / what to ask for)

1. **Excluded-studies list (AMSTAR-2 #7, PRISMA #16b) — CONFIRMED MISSING.** No such list exists in the article or SI; this is the sole driver of the AMSTAR-2 "Low". Only the authors can close it (by publishing the list). *This is the one item between "Low" and "Moderate/High".*
2. **Search breadth (AMSTAR-2 #4) — CONFIRMED Partial Yes.** SI S2 confirms 3 databases with full reproducible strategies and no language limit, but no trial-registry, grey-literature, or reference-list searching. A future update adding those would lift item 4 to full "Yes".
3. **Protocol amendments (PRISMA #24c, AMSTAR-2 #2).** No statement on deviations from the registered PROSPERO protocol. A one-line "no amendments" or a list would close this.
4. **Review-process limitations (PRISMA #23c).** Limitations focus on the evidence (pooled comparators, sensor heterogeneity); a brief note on review-process limitations (e.g., no independent duplicate of the search itself, reliance on author-supplied unpublished data) would complete the item.

## 6. Note on the promotional card (US-DBA-2600233)

The Medtronic evidence card accurately reproduces the headline findings (MiniMed 780G largest TIR improvement; DBLG1 largest TBR reduction; low serious-AE rates) **and — to its credit — reproduces the primary study's own caution** that "the certainty of the evidence was low for many comparisons… should not be regarded as conclusive." It is a faithful summary. It is **not** itself a systematic review and was not appraised against PRISMA/ROBIS/AMSTAR-2.
